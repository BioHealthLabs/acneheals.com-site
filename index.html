How to choose the right acne treatment

Stronger is not always better. Throwing every active at your face at once is one of the fastest ways to wreck your barrier.

  • Beginners: start with a gentle cleanser and one active (BHA or BPO).
  • Oilier, inflamed skin: benzoyl peroxide can be very effective.
  • Clogged pores/texture: salicylic acid (BHA) is often a good first step.
  • Persistent acne: adapalene (Differin) may help long-term – but go slow.

What to avoid if you are acne-prone

Many “acne” products still contain irritating extras that make things worse.

  • Harsh physical scrubs with large, abrasive particles.
  • Strong fragrance, essential oils and high alcohol content.
  • Heavy, occlusive creams marketed as “non-comedogenic” with no testing behind it.

Acne Treatment FAQ

How long does it take to see results from acne treatments?

Most people see small improvements in 4–6 weeks and clearer changes in 8–12 weeks. Retinoids like adapalene often need 3–6 months for full effect.

Can I use benzoyl peroxide and retinoids together?

Some routines combine them (e.g. BPO cleanser in the morning, adapalene at night), but it can be too much for sensitive skin. Introduce one product at a time and watch your barrier closely.

When should I see a dermatologist?

If you have deep, painful cysts, scarring, or if OTC products have not helped after a few months, it is time to speak to a professional. Severe acne is not something you need to “tough out” alone.
