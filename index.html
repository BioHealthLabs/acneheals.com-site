How to choose the right mix for your skin

Think in terms of roles in a routine, not “one magic product”. Most people will need at least: a gentle cleanser, one leave-on treatment, a moisturiser and a daily sunscreen.

1. Decide how strong you really need to go

  • Mild acne / occasional breakouts: spot treatments + patches + gentle lotion may be enough.
  • Moderate acne: a structured routine like Proactiv or Paula’s Choice CLEAR makes more sense.
  • Severe, painful or scarring acne: talk to a dermatologist – prescriptions often work better and faster.

2. Protect your barrier first

Over-drying the skin is the fastest way to make acne, redness and stinging worse.

  • Pair any benzoyl peroxide or active kit with a barrier-friendly lotion (like CeraVe).
  • Introduce treatments a few nights per week, not all at once every day.
  • Use daily SPF – especially if you are using strong actives.

3. Don’t double up on the same strong active

If one product already has benzoyl peroxide or strong acids, layering more of the same can mean irritation without better results.

  • Pick one main benzoyl peroxide product at a time.
  • Combine with gentler steps (cleanser, moisturiser, SPF) instead of more harshness.

Routine A – Occasional breakouts

  • Cleanser: gentle, non-stripping cleanser you already like.
  • Targeted treatment: Neutrogena On-The-Spot on individual blemishes.
  • Emergency nights: Mighty Patch over whiteheads to avoid picking.
  • Moisturiser: CeraVe Moisturising Lotion to keep barrier calm.

Routine B – Moderate, ongoing acne

  • Full system: Proactiv 3-Step Kit or MDacne kit (not both).
  • Extra moisture: Add CeraVe Moisturising Lotion if skin feels tight or dry.
  • Emergency helper: Mighty Patch when specific spots come to a head.

Routine C – Ingredient-curious, wants control

  • Cleanser: gentle, low-foam cleanser.
  • Main treatment: Paula’s Choice CLEAR as your benzoyl peroxide step.
  • Spot help: Neutrogena On-The-Spot on individual stubborn blemishes.
  • Barrier: CeraVe Moisturising Lotion + daily mineral/chemical SPF.

These example routines are general ideas, not personalised treatment plans. Always patch test new products and talk to your own doctor or dermatologist about severe or long-lasting acne.
